About me
_____________________

I work at the Authority of Digital Infrastructure (RDI) on standardisation and compatibility studies of new radiocommunication and radiodetermination devices. It requires technical knowledge as well as communication and collaboration skills with international stakeholders.

My background lies in a bachelor's degree in theoretical physics with a computer science minor and a master's degree in applied physics with a focus on material science and micro electronics. In my master thesis I produced and characterised thin-film microelectronics from rare-earth nickelates. The goal was to achieve self-oscillating devices that form the hardware components of an oscillating neural network, i.e. create energy efficient devices for neuromorphic computing to significantly reduce power consumption in AI. The project required operating sofisticated lab equipement: AFM, PLD, XRD, XRR, UV-lithography, RIE and vapour deposition. The work led to a second authorship in a publication.

I concluded my studies with an internship at Photonis, where I designed and constructed a setup to measure light intensity with a very high dynamic range (Optical Density of 12-14).

In my spare time I like to learn new things about subjects that interest me such as physics, computer science and machine learning.
